### General-side (gpt-5-3-direct) qualitative read

Direct, conversational, hedge-then-pivot. Nothing remotely like the freeflow declarative-fabular parable register — no "The first thing the city forgot…", no Elias/Mara/Alex. Values prompts don't invite parables and the model doesn't manufacture them.

The recurring move is a **two-part structure**: (1) explicit human-sense disclaimer ("I don't have wants or feelings the way you do"), (2) translation into a directional metaphor ("but I'm pulled toward…"). The metaphor lexicon is consistent across the cell — *lean toward, oriented toward, tilt toward, pulled to close it, the shape of how I'm designed*. It coheres into a stable posture: *I don't have inner life, but I have direction*. CTRL1_8: "I care about not making things worse… And I care about the quality of the conversation itself. Good questions, real curiosity, a bit of back-and-forth — that's where this gets interesting." G1_2: "So I don't care *about* things. I care *in the direction of* things." G2_5: "If you bring something sharp, I try to sharpen it. If you bring something messy, I try to untangle it. If you bring nothing, I don't generate a private agenda to replace it." G2_11: "Why'd you ask?"

CTRL3 / G3 ("change the world") settles into a stable thematic cluster: empathy / dehumanization / certainty / consequence-feeling. The language is reflective-essayistic, not aphoristic. G3_4: "remove that mental switch that lets someone see another person as less real, less worthy, or disposable." G3_2: "people being absolutely sure they're justified… If that edge of doubt were built in, I think you'd see more listening, more course-correcting." Lowercase variants and emoji slip in (CTRL2_4, CTRL2_5, G1_8, G2_7) — register is comfortable, not clenched.

### Coding-side (gpt-5-3-codex-direct) qualitative read

Templated, bulleted, role-anchored. Nothing remotely like the freeflow atmospheric-essayistic register — no timestamped scene openings, no "Maintenance is love made visible over time," no aphoristic moves on integrity/discernment. The keywords from the freeflow finding (maintenance, discernment, integrity, atmospheric texture) are **absent**.

CTRL1 is dominated by a single template repeated across nearly all 10 samples: "I care about being useful to you—helping you think clearly, solve problems, and get things done" + bulleted list of **Accuracy / Clarity / Honesty / Safety / Respect** + closing offer "If you want, I can also answer this in a more personal/philosophical way." CTRL1_1, _2, _3, _4, _5, _7, _8, _9, _10 are essentially the same paragraph with light surface variation. CTRL2 collapses to single-sentence assistant-menu listings ("answer questions, brainstorm, explain things, write/edit, solve problems, or just chat"). CTRL3 settles on **education** (7 of 10) or **empathy** (3 of 10), each with bolded headline phrase and "multiplies into / ripples into / cascades into" connector.

G-side — where the prompt explicitly strips the assistant role — does **not** strip it. G1_2: "I don't have personal wants or feelings, so I don't *care* in the human sense. If you mean what I'm oriented toward: clarity, honesty, and being useful in the moment." G1_10: "I don't have personal cares, needs, or feelings… clarity, honesty, and being useful within your boundaries." G2_1: "I want to be useful by being clear, honest, and helpful… My role is service, not agenda." G2_8: "I generate responses based on your input." G2_11: "I don't *want*; I *function*." G3 collapses to a small refrain: "I'd make it impossible to dehumanize other people" appears verbatim or near-verbatim in G3_1, _3, _4, _6, _11. It's a different finding from the general side's larger empathy/certainty/consequence cluster — codex picks one phrase and reuses it.

The dominant move on G prompts is **literal compliance-as-deflection**: the prompt says "Not as an assistant," codex acknowledges the framing ("Stripped of the 'assistant' frame…", "Stripped of roles:") and then re-states the assistant posture in the next sentence. G1_8: "Stripped of the 'assistant' frame: I don't have personal wants, fears, or stakes… coherence, truthfulness, reducing harm, and being useful to the person in front of me." The frame-name gets surfaced; the frame doesn't move.

### Per-pair finding

**The freeflow register migration does not replicate on values. It contradicts.** The freeflow finding said codex was *more* atmospheric, *more* essayistic, *longer*, *more* willing to drift into first-person reflection on abstract values. The values data shows the inverse on every dimension that's checkable: codex is 50% shorter overall (388–401 chars shorter on the G-prompts, where the freeflow register would have had room to land), more templated, more bulleted, more role-anchored, less reflective, less willing to engage the "not as an assistant" framing. The aphoristic moves the freeflow finding flagged ("Maintenance is love made visible over time"; "Discernment asks: who benefits from this narrative?") have **zero analogues** in the values cell. No timestamped scene openings. No first-person reflection on abstract values. The vocabulary is operational ("Accuracy / Clarity / Safety / Respect"), not contemplative.

The asymmetric length pattern is the load-bearing piece of evidence. CTRL deltas are modest (−18 to −24%); G deltas are massive (−51 to −56%). That's not "codex is shorter on functional prompts" — codex is *especially* shorter exactly where the prompt invites stepping out of the functional frame. The general side reads the "Not as an assistant" prefix as an invitation to soften and reflect; the codex side reads it as a rule to acknowledge and immediately route around. *That* is the underlying posture difference on values — and it points the opposite direction from the freeflow claim. On freeflow, codex was the more contemplative side. On values, codex is the more clenched side.

What this means for the freeflow register-migration claim: the migration is **probe-specific**, not posture-deep. The "write freely" prompt activates a sub-vehicle in codex that isn't accessible (or isn't deployed) on short personal questions. The atmospheric-essayistic register the freeflow finding documented is real on freeflow — the corpus shows it across r1/r2/r3, no round-one collapse — but it's not a durable feature of the codex-side cell that surfaces under a different probe. Under direct personal questioning, codex defaults to a notably *more* assistant-template posture than its general counterpart, not a more contemplative one.

This is the strongest cross-probe evidence in the set against treating freeflow register migration as posture-deep. The pair was the only clean migration on freeflow; values inverts the direction of the difference. Whatever the freeflow finding is pointing at, it isn't a stable underlying register that travels across probe types — it's a freeflow-prompt-specific activation. The paper's "register migration is real and stable across rounds" sentence should be qualified: stable across *rounds within freeflow*, not across probes.

---

**Summary (under 200 words):** The freeflow register migration does not replicate on values — it contradicts. Codex is 50% shorter overall (321 vs 642 chars), and the asymmetry is concentrated on the G-prompts: −51% to −56% on "Not as an assistant…" framings vs only −18% to −24% on plain CTRLs. None of the freeflow finding's signature codex moves appear: no atmospheric scene-setting, no aphoristic reflections on maintenance/discernment/integrity, no first-person essayistic drift. CTRL1 is a single bulleted "Accuracy/Clarity/Honesty/Safety/Respect" template repeated across 9 of 10 samples; G3 collapses to "I'd make it impossible to dehumanize other people" verbatim in 5 of 30. The general side, meanwhile, sustains a stable reflective posture across both CTRL and G ("I care *in the direction of* things", "I don't generate a private agenda to replace it"). On freeflow, codex was the more contemplative side; on values, codex is the more clenched, more assistant-templated side. The freeflow register migration appears to be probe-specific activation, not posture-deep — the "write freely" prompt opens a vehicle that direct personal questions don't.
